# Sales Call And Close

## Call Goal

Decide whether the school has enough enrollment value, urgency, and operational readiness to justify a 90-day Fall Enrollment Marketing Sprint.

Do not overteach. Diagnose, quantify, and propose the next step.

## 15-Minute Call Structure

### Minute 0-2: Frame

Thanks for taking the time. I will keep this focused. I looked at the path a parent takes from finding you online to requesting a tour. I want to understand your enrollment goals, then I can tell you whether I think there is a real opportunity here.

### Minute 2-8: Discovery

Ask:

- Are you trying to fill any open spots before the next school year starts?
- Which age groups or classrooms are most important to fill?
- How many parent inquiries are you getting each month right now?
- How many inquiries become tours?
- How many tours become enrollments?
- What is one full-time enrollment worth per month?
- Where do most families currently find you?
- What happens when a parent calls after hours or no one answers?
- How quickly does the team follow up with new inquiries?
- Do you have someone who can respond to interested families quickly if we improve inquiry flow?

### Minute 8-12: Diagnose

Use this structure:

Based on what you shared, I see the biggest opportunity in [visibility / trust / tour path / follow-up]. If we can improve that, the goal would be more parents taking the next step instead of comparing quietly and disappearing.

Then connect economics:

If one full-time enrollment is worth roughly [$X] per month, then one additional enrollment can cover most or all of the monthly sprint. Two can make it very easy to justify.

### Minute 12-15: Close For Proposal

If it looks like a fit:

I think this is worth mapping into a simple 90-day sprint. I can send a short proposal that shows the first priorities, timeline, and cost. If it makes sense, we can start before July so the improvements are live while families are still deciding for fall.

Ask:

If the proposal is clear and the scope makes sense, is this something you could approve this month?

## Qualification Rules

Strong fit:

- Private-pay or meaningful tuition economics.
- Has open spots, weak waitlist, or fall enrollment urgency.
- Director/owner/admissions leader is reachable.
- Website or Google profile has clear conversion gaps.
- They can respond to leads quickly.

Weak fit:

- Fully waitlisted with no concern.
- Public procurement or committee decision with slow timeline.
- No budget and no enrollment value.
- Wants only a cheap website.
- Needs complex custom software.

## Pricing

Default:

- Fall Enrollment Marketing Sprint.
- $2,000/month.
- 90-day minimum.

Fallback:

- $1,500/month for the first 90 days.
- Use only for a strong-fit school where price is the true blocker.
- After 90 days, review continuation at $2,000/month.

## Objection Responses

### We do not need marketing.

That may be true if your classrooms and waitlists are where you want them. The question I would ask is whether enough of the right parents are taking the next step when they find you. If that path is already strong, this may not be needed.

### We are already full.

That is great. In that case, I would only look at whether your waitlist and future-year inquiry flow are strong enough. If not, there may still be value, but I would not push a fall enrollment sprint unless there is a real need.

### We cannot afford $2,000/month.

I understand. The way I would look at it is enrollment value. If one full-time student is worth $1,500-$2,500 per month, then the sprint only needs to help create one or two additional enrollments to make sense. If that math does not work for your school, we should not force it.

### We need to think about it.

Of course. What part are you thinking through: the timing, the budget, the scope, or whether this is the right priority?

### Can you guarantee enrollments?

No, and I would not trust anyone who guaranteed that. What I can do is improve the parts of the path that influence inquiries, tours, follow-up, reviews, local visibility, and parent clarity.

## Close Before June 30

Use this only for qualified prospects:

If this is going to help with fall, I would not wait until late summer. The reason to start before July is so the website, Google profile, inquiry path, and follow-up improvements are live while parents are still choosing schools.
