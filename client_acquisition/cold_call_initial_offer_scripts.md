# Cold Call Scripts For Initial Offers

Use these as call tracks, not word-for-word monologues. The goal is to quickly identify the business type, current enrollment bottleneck, and whether the low-ticket entry offer makes sense.

## Core Positioning

School Enrollment Partners helps childcare providers stop losing parent inquiries by tightening the path from Google, phone calls, forms, follow-up, reviews, tours, and waitlists.

The first sale is not "more leads." The first sale is "capture and follow up with the parent demand you already have."

## Universal Front Desk Opener

Hi, this is [Your Name] with School Enrollment Partners. I had a quick question for whoever handles enrollment, tours, or parent inquiries. Is that the owner or director?

If asked what it is about:

I help childcare programs catch parent inquiries that usually slip through the cracks, like missed calls, slow follow-up, weak review flow, or waitlist follow-up. I wanted to ask one quick question and see if it is relevant.

If they ask if this is sales:

Yes, but I will keep it direct. If you are full and your inquiry follow-up is already tight, it may not be relevant.

## Quick Qualification Questions

Ask 2-4 depending on how much time they give you:

- Are you currently full, or do you have openings?
- When a parent calls and no one answers, does anything automatically text them back?
- Do new parent inquiries go into a system, or are they mostly handled by phone, email, and memory?
- Are you actively trying to get more reviews from happy families?
- Do you have a waitlist you actively follow up with, or does it mostly sit there?
- Are tours easy for parents to book, or does someone have to coordinate everything manually?

## Offer 1: In-Home Day Care

Initial offer: Warm Waitlist  
Price: $127/month + $149 setup

### Best Prospect

- In-home provider
- Usually full or close to full
- Has occasional openings
- Cannot justify a big marketing retainer
- Needs reviews, missed-call backup, inquiry capture, or waitlist follow-up

### Opener

Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with School Enrollment Partners. I work with childcare providers on the parent inquiry and waitlist side.

I know in-home providers usually do not need some big marketing package, especially if you are full. The reason I am calling is we have a small Warm Waitlist setup that helps keep reviews, parent inquiries, missed calls, and future openings organized.

Can I ask, are you full right now, or do you have any openings coming up?

### If They Are Full

That is actually where this can make sense. I would not tell you to pay for heavy marketing if you are full.

The idea is just to keep things warm: collect reviews, capture new parent interest, send basic follow-up, and keep a small waitlist ready so that when one spot opens, you are not starting from zero.

It is $127/month after a $149 setup. Very lightweight.

Would it be worth taking 10 minutes to see if your current waitlist and follow-up process is already strong enough?

### If They Have An Opening

Got it. For one opening, I would still keep this simple. The Warm Waitlist setup helps capture interested parents, follow up automatically, and keep reviews working for you.

If the opening is urgent, there is also a separate spot-fill push, but I would not start there unless you need it.

Would it be useful if I showed you what the basic setup would look like?

### Discovery

- How many children are you licensed for?
- How often do openings usually come up?
- Do you have a written waitlist right now?
- How do parents usually find you?
- Do you text parents back manually when they inquire?
- Are you asking current families for Google reviews?

### Close

This sounds like a fit for the Warm Waitlist package, not a big marketing plan.

The setup is $149, then $127/month. It keeps reviews, inquiries, missed-call follow-up, and waitlist follow-up organized so you are ready when a spot opens.

Would you want me to send the simple setup outline?

### Objections

#### I am full.

That is exactly why I would keep this small. I am not suggesting lead generation. I am suggesting a low-cost system to keep future demand warm so openings are easier to fill.

#### I do not want to pay monthly.

Totally fair. The reason it is monthly is because reviews, inquiries, text follow-up, and waitlist nurturing keep running. If you only need help for one opening, the one-time spot-fill sprint may fit better.

#### I already have a waitlist.

That is good. The question is whether parents on that waitlist are being followed up with and kept warm, or whether they are quietly finding care somewhere else.

## Offer 2: Small / Medium Childcare Center

Initial offer: Enrollment Foundation  
Price: $349/month + $349 setup

### Best Prospect

- Small or medium center
- Has a director or admin person
- Gets parent inquiries but follow-up is messy
- Misses calls
- Needs reviews
- Has tours, openings, or waitlist management issues
- Not ready for a $1,500-$3,000/month marketing package

### Opener

Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with School Enrollment Partners. I help childcare centers tighten the parent inquiry process before spending more money on ads or marketing.

The reason I am calling is most centers are not losing families because they need a giant marketing package. They are losing families because calls get missed, follow-up is slow, reviews are not being requested, or interested parents are not moved cleanly toward a tour.

Can I ask, when a new parent inquiry comes in, do you have a system that tracks it and follows up automatically?

### If They Say No / Not Really

That is the exact gap we usually start with.

Our entry package is called Enrollment Foundation. It is $349/month after a $349 setup. It sets up the basic system: parent lead capture, missed-call text-back, automated follow-up, review requests, basic pipeline, and waitlist nurture.

It is intentionally not a big expensive marketing package. It is the foundation before adding more traffic.

Would it be worth a quick 10-minute call to see where parent inquiries are leaking right now?

### If They Say They Already Have A System

That is good. Then the question is usually whether it is actually being used and whether parents are getting followed up with quickly.

Do you know roughly how many inquiries become tours each month?

If they know:

Great. Then we can look at whether the bottleneck is lead capture, tour booking, no-shows, or follow-up.

If they do not know:

That is usually the first thing to fix. If you cannot see the pipeline, it is hard to know whether marketing is working.

### Discovery

- How many locations do you have?
- Are you currently trying to fill any classrooms or age groups?
- About how many parent inquiries come in each month?
- How many tours happen from those inquiries?
- How quickly does someone follow up?
- What happens when the phone is missed?
- Are review requests automated?
- Is your waitlist actively nurtured?

### Close

Based on what you shared, I would not start with ads. I would start with the Enrollment Foundation system.

It is $349 setup and $349/month. The goal is to capture parent demand, respond faster, improve review flow, and make the inquiry-to-tour path visible.

If that foundation works, then later we can look at tour booking, AI response, local SEO, or ads. But I would not upsell those until the basic system is working.

Would you want me to send the one-page outline?

### Objections

#### We are full.

That is great. If you are full across every room and the waitlist is strong, you may only need the lighter waitlist/review side of this. The question is whether your future openings are protected or whether families disappear when they do not hear from you.

#### We already get leads.

That may be true. The issue is usually not just lead volume. It is whether leads are captured, followed up with, booked for tours, and tracked. If that is already strong, you may not need us.

#### We do not want ads.

I agree. I would not start with ads. This offer is the system before ads: missed-call text-back, follow-up, reviews, pipeline, and waitlist nurture.

#### $349/month is still a cost.

It is. The way I would think about it is that one enrollment is usually worth far more than the monthly cost. But the first goal is not a promise of new enrollments. It is to stop losing the parent interest you already have.

## Offer 3: Large Center / Multi-Site

Initial offer: Enrollment Ops Base  
Price: $3,500/month + $2,500 implementation

### Best Prospect

- Larger single site or multi-site operator
- Has staff and operational complexity
- Multiple rooms, age groups, or locations
- Needs visibility into pipeline and capacity
- Can act on reports
- Has enough enrollment economics to justify premium support

### Opener

Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with School Enrollment Partners. I work with childcare operators on enrollment operations: inquiry routing, follow-up, tours, reviews, waitlists, and pipeline visibility by classroom or location.

I am not calling about a basic ads package. For larger centers, the bigger issue is usually knowing where demand is coming from, which rooms actually need families, and whether parent inquiries are being followed up with consistently.

Can I ask, do you currently track enrollment pipeline by classroom or location?

### If They Do Not Track Clearly

That is usually where we help.

For larger operators, we start with an Enrollment Ops Base implementation. It builds the intake, routing, pipeline, follow-up, review workflow, and reporting layer so leadership can see what is happening before openings become revenue problems.

It starts at $3,500/month with a $2,500 implementation.

Is that kind of enrollment visibility something you are already working on this quarter?

### If They Already Track It

That is good. Then I would be curious where the weak point is.

Is the bigger issue lead volume, response speed, tour conversion, no-shows, waitlist management, or knowing which classrooms need demand?

### Discovery

- How many locations or classrooms are you managing?
- Do openings vary by age group?
- Who owns parent follow-up?
- Do you know inquiry-to-tour and tour-to-enrollment conversion?
- Are no-shows tracked?
- Are reviews requested consistently by location?
- Is reporting centralized?
- Are campaigns tied to actual capacity?

### Close

It sounds like this is less about buying ads and more about enrollment operations.

The Enrollment Ops Base is $2,500 to implement and $3,500/month. It gives you lead routing, pipeline by classroom or location, automated follow-up, review workflows, and a monthly enrollment performance review.

If it looks useful, the next step would be a 30-minute enrollment ops audit where we map where demand is leaking and what needs to be systematized first.

Would it make sense to schedule that with whoever owns enrollment and operations?

### Objections

#### That is expensive.

It is a premium offer, so it only makes sense if the enrollment economics justify it. For larger operators, one classroom gap or repeated slow follow-up can cost much more than the system. If that is not true in your case, I would not push it.

#### We already have marketing.

That is fine. This is not meant to replace marketing automatically. It is the operating layer underneath it: routing, follow-up, pipeline, reviews, tour movement, and capacity visibility.

#### We are full.

That is good, but for larger operators I would still ask whether you are full in the right rooms, at every site, and whether your future pipeline is visible. "Full today" and "protected from openings next month" are different things.

#### Send me information.

Happy to. I can send a short outline, but it will be more useful if I know whether you are managing one site, multiple classrooms, or multiple locations. Which is it?

If in-home:
