# School Enrollment Partners Offer Ladder

Updated: June 21, 2026

## What This File Is For

This file is the internal offer outline for School Enrollment Partners. It explains what is being sold to each type of school or childcare provider, what the client gets in the initial offer, what is intentionally left out, and which add-ons can be added later when the client needs more support.

This is not meant to be a public pricing page. It is a working sales and fulfillment reference so that future proposals, website pages, sales calls, and delivery checklists stay consistent.

The main idea is simple:

1. Start each client on the right initial offer for their size and complexity.
2. Do not overload the first offer with every possible service.
3. Watch for the actual bottleneck after the initial system is in place.
4. Add services when the client needs them.
5. For in-home providers, remove temporary add-ons when they no longer need them and return them to the base offer.
6. For small, medium, and large schools, use the initial offer as the foundation and keep expanding the account over time when it makes sense.

## Core Pricing Principle

The first sale should not be framed as "more leads." The first sale should be framed as "stop leaking parent demand."

Most schools and childcare providers already have some parent interest. The problem is that inquiries are missed, calls go unanswered, follow-up is inconsistent, reviews are not being requested, waitlists are not being nurtured, and directors do not have a clear view of what is happening.

School Enrollment Partners sells the system that captures, organizes, follows up with, and tracks parent demand before the client spends more money trying to create new demand.

The offer ladder should be built around three client types:

| Client Type | Sales Strategy | Pricing Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| In-home day care | Low-cost base offer with temporary add-ons when needed | Keep them simple, affordable, and easy to scale back down |
| Small/medium center | Strong initial operating foundation, then upsell bottleneck fixes | Start with the core system, then expand over time |
| Large center / multi-site operator | Premium enrollment operations base, then strategic expansion | Start high because the work requires judgment, reporting, and management |

## How The Ladder Should Be Sold

### In-home providers

In-home providers should not be sold a large ongoing marketing package first. They usually need a simple base system that keeps them organized, helps them capture demand, and builds trust with reviews.

The base offer should be affordable enough that they can keep it running even when they are full. When they need extra help, add temporary services. When they no longer need the extra service, remove it and return them to the base Warm Waitlist offer.

Example:

- Provider starts on Warm Waitlist at $127/mo.
- They suddenly have one infant opening and need to fill it.
- Add Spot Fill Sprint as a one-time push.
- Once the opening is filled, they go back to Warm Waitlist.

This keeps the account useful year-round without forcing the provider to pay for services they do not always need.

### Small and medium centers

Small and medium centers should start with the Enrollment Foundation. The goal is to make sure parent inquiries are captured, missed calls are recovered, review requests are automated, waitlist families are followed up with, and the director can see the enrollment pipeline.

After that, upsell based on the actual bottleneck:

- If they get inquiries but not tours, add Tour Booking System.
- If leads are messy, add CRM Pipeline Upgrade.
- If no-shows are a problem, add No-Show Recovery.
- If local visibility is weak, add Local SEO Visibility.
- If intake is stable and they can handle more demand, add Paid Ads Management Lite.

This tier should grow through practical expansion, not through selling everything upfront.

### Large centers and multi-site operators

Large centers and multi-site operators should not start on a low-ticket package. They usually need routing, reporting, accountability, and strategic support. The base offer should be premium because the work includes operational judgment, not just automations.

The Enrollment Ops Base gives leadership a managed enrollment layer. From there, expansion should focus on reporting, capacity planning, multi-site visibility, paid acquisition, staff response improvement, and quarterly strategy work.

## Initial Offer 1: Warm Waitlist

Business type: In-home day care  
Price: $127/month  
Setup fee: $149

### Who This Is For

Warm Waitlist is for in-home childcare providers who need a simple, affordable system to capture parent interest and avoid losing families when they are busy or full.

This is the best fit when the provider:

- Has limited capacity.
- May be full today but wants future openings easier to fill.
- Misses calls while caring for children.
- Has parent inquiries scattered across voicemail, text, email, and memory.
- Needs more reviews but does not want to manually ask every family.
- Does not need a full marketing campaign every month.

### Core Promise

Warm Waitlist helps an in-home provider stop losing interested parents by giving them a simple system for missed-call recovery, inquiry capture, waitlist follow-up, and review requests.

### What The Customer Gets

- A lightweight HighLevel sub-account configured for the provider.
- One tracking/local phone number for missed-call text-back.
- Simple parent inquiry form.
- Basic waitlist capture form.
- Missed-call text-back workflow.
- New inquiry auto-reply text.
- New inquiry follow-up email.
- Basic waitlist nurture sequence.
- Review request workflow for happy families.
- Simple monthly snapshot showing inquiries, review requests, and waitlist activity.

### What This Does For Them

- Parents who call and do not get an answer can still receive a quick text response.
- Interested families have a simple way to ask about openings or join a waitlist.
- The provider is not relying only on memory, voicemail, or scattered texts.
- Happy families can be asked for reviews more consistently.
- Future openings are easier to fill because interested parents are being captured and followed up with.
- The provider can stay visible and ready without paying for a full marketing campaign.

### What Is Not Included

- Paid ads.
- AI Employee.
- Local SEO / Search Atlas.
- Online Listings.
- Manual lead calling.
- Custom website build.
- Full CRM pipeline buildout.
- Tour booking system.
- Ongoing content posting.
- Ongoing campaign management.

### Setup Work Covered By The $149 Setup Fee

- Create or configure the HighLevel sub-account.
- Connect one phone number.
- Build the parent inquiry form.
- Build the waitlist form.
- Build missed-call text-back.
- Build review request workflow.
- Build basic follow-up workflow.
- Test form submission, text, and email flow.

### In-Home Add-On Strategy

For in-home providers, add-ons should usually be temporary, situational, or easy to remove. The goal is to keep the provider on the base Warm Waitlist offer long term and only add extra services when there is a specific reason.

| Situation | Add-On | How To Use It | What Happens After |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| One opening needs to be filled | Spot Fill Sprint | Run a focused one-time push around the opening | Remove after the opening is filled |
| Parents inquire but do not schedule a visit | Tour Booking System | Add a simple booking path and reminders | Keep only if ongoing tour scheduling is needed |
| Provider has weak reviews | Review Push Campaign | Run a short review request push to past happy families | Return to normal review workflow after campaign |
| Provider wants more local visibility | Local Visibility Mini | Clean up basic listings and profile information | Keep only if ongoing visibility work is justified |
| Provider is getting too many repetitive questions | AI Parent Response Assistant | Add AI help for common parent questions | Remove if usage is low or provider is full |

## Initial Offer 3: Enrollment Ops Base

Business type: Large center / multi-site operator  
Price: $3,500/month

Enrollment Ops Base is for larger childcare centers, larger Montessori schools, private school groups, and multi-site operators that need a managed enrollment operations layer instead of a simple automation package.

This is the best fit when the operator:

- Has multiple classrooms, programs, or locations.
- Needs visibility into where demand is coming from.
- Needs routing by location, classroom, age group, or program.
- Has multiple staff members involved in intake and follow-up.
- Needs strategy and reporting, not just software setup.
- Wants to understand whether the bottleneck is demand, response speed, tour conversion, no-shows, or capacity mismatch.

### Core Promise

Enrollment Ops Base gives leadership a managed enrollment operations system so they can see demand, route inquiries, improve follow-up, request reviews, nurture waitlists, and make better enrollment decisions.

- HighLevel workspace/sub-account structure for the operator.

- Tour reminder workflow.
- Review request workflow.
- Waitlist follow-up workflow.
- Basic enrollment performance reporting.
- Monthly strategy review.
- Bottleneck roadmap for upsells or operational improvements.

## Margin Rules

- The agency HighLevel plan should be treated as a fixed overhead allocated across active clients.
- Client messaging and AI usage should either be priced into the package or rebilled with margin.
- Do not include AI Employee in a low-ticket package unless the price reflects the sub-account AI cost.
- In-home providers can be low-ticket, but they still need enough margin to cover a real HighLevel sub-account footprint.
- In-home add-ons should usually be temporary unless the provider clearly needs the service every month.
- Small/medium centers should enter at $349/month, then move into HighLevel add-ons as bottlenecks appear.
- Large operators should start premium because the work includes strategy, reporting, and operational judgment.
- Paid ad spend is separate from management fees unless a proposal explicitly says otherwise.
- Manual work should be scoped tightly because labor is what compresses margins.
